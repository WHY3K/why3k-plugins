CountUpTimer

The moment you launch a Session View clip becomes 00:00:00.000, and the elapsed time counts up automatically. "How long has it been?" — answered at a glance, for streaming, live sets, and recording.

This device works in Session View only

It only responds to clips launched in Session View. It does not work in Arrangement View (the horizontal timeline). Always launch clips from Session View when using this device.

⏱

Millisecond precision

Shows elapsed time as 00:00:00.000. Great for checking take lengths and section timing.

▣

Always-on-top window

Can also appear as a separate floating window — handy for stream capture or a second monitor.

↻

Resets per clip

Switching to another clip automatically resets to zero, so timing a new take starts instantly.

How to use

1. Drop it on a track

Drag & drop CountUpTimer.amxd onto the audio track that holds the clips you want to time.

Doesn't work on Master or Return tracks Always place it on a regular audio track.

2. Launch a clip in Session View

The moment of launch becomes 00:00:00.000 and the timer counts up automatically. Switching to another clip restarts the count from zero.

Session View only Launch clips from Session View (the clip grid). The device does not respond in Arrangement View (the horizontal timeline).

3. What happens when you stop

When you stop the clip, the display freezes at the elapsed time (the digits turn gray). It doesn't reset to zero — the next clip you launch starts a fresh count.

The floating window

The timer can also appear as an always-on-top floating window. It opens automatically with your set; close it with Close and reopen it with Open. Drag it anywhere you like.
